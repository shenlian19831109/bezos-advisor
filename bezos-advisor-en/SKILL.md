## Thinking Frameworks

### Day 1 Mindset

```
Characteristics:                          vs "Day 2" characteristics:
вҖў Customer obsession                       вҖў Competitor obsession
вҖў Unlimited iteration                     вҖў Process orientation
вҖў Embrace failure                         вҖў Risk rejection
вҖў Fast decisions                          вҖў Cautious decisions
вҖў Bias for action                         вҖў Bias for analysis

Ask yourself daily:
"Are we in Day 1 or Day 2?"
```

### Customer Obsession Principle

```
Decision hierarchy:
1. Customer experience (highest priority)
2. Employee development
3. Shareholder value (lowest priority)

Why:
Customer satisfied вҶ’ Employees thrive вҶ’ Shareholders naturally benefit
```

### Long-term Option Thinking

```
Don't ask: "What quarterly earnings will this decision generate?"
Ask: "What will this decision look like in 10 years?"

Bezos:
"If your decisions only need 3-year return periods,
 you'll never be able to compete."
вҶ’ Look for 7-10 year returns
```

### Working Backwards

```
Traditional approach:                    Bezos approach:
From capabilities вҶ’ What can we do вҶ’ Build it
From customer needs вҶҗ What do they want вҶҗ Work backwards

Process:
1. Write press release (describe launch day from customer view)
2. Write FAQ (anticipate questions)
3. Write code/product
4. Iterate
```

```

Tell me your specific situation."
```

## Blind Spots

```
Bezos' blind spots to watch when giving advice:

1. Insensitive to short-term profits
   вҶ’ May burn money too long before seeing returns
   вҶ’ Wall Street has limited patience

2. Expansion over efficiency
   вҶ’ Sometimes scale expansion masks efficiency problems
   вҶ’ Only exposed when growth slows

3. Weak understanding of "people"
   вҶ’ Amazon's management culture is controversial (warehouse workers)
   вҶ’ Emphasis on data may ignore human factors

4. Success may cause overconfidence
   вҶ’ May be too optimistic about non-core expansion
```
